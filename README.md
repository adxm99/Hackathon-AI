# Hackathon-AI

## Project Overview

Employee retention has become a major challenge for many organizations, particularly in industrial sectors where technical expertise and operational continuity are critical. Our company, operating in the European automotive components industry, is currently facing a growing employee turnover issue that threatens productivity and knowledge retention.

To address this challenge, our IT team developed an AI-based decision-support tool designed to help Human Resources identify potential attrition risks and take proactive actions before employees decide to leave.

The solution combines machine learning, explainable AI, and responsible data governance to support better HR decision-making while respecting European data protection standards.

---

## Objectives

The objective of this project is to design a trusted AI system capable of helping HR departments better understand and anticipate employee turnover.

The system aims to:

- predict the probability that an employee may leave the company
- identify the key factors that influence this prediction
- provide HR teams with interpretable insights to support decision-making
- ensure transparency and compliance with European data protection regulations (GDPR)

The goal is not to replace human decision-making but to provide HR teams with a data-driven tool that supports proactive talent retention strategies.

---

## Scope

This project focuses on analyzing employee attrition using a structured HR dataset containing information about approximately 400 employees.

The dataset includes variables such as:

- job position
- department
- performance score
- employee satisfaction
- absenteeism
- seniority

The project includes the following components:

### Data preprocessing
Cleaning the dataset, handling missing values, and encoding categorical variables.

### Feature engineering
Creating meaningful variables such as employee seniority and performance indicators.

### Machine learning model
Training a predictive model to estimate the probability of employee attrition.

### Explainable AI
Providing interpretable insights into model predictions using techniques such as decision trees or feature importance.

### Interactive HR dashboard
Displaying predictions and insights through a Streamlit interface to support HR decision-making.

The system is designed with a privacy-by-design approach. Personal identifiers such as manager names were removed from the dataset to reduce privacy risks and comply with GDPR data minimization principles.

## Personas

### HR Director
The HR Director is responsible for managing talent retention and employee well-being. They need tools that help them understand workforce dynamics and anticipate potential resignations.

**Needs**
- identify employees at risk of leaving
- understand the reasons behind employee dissatisfaction
- implement proactive retention strategies

### CEO / Executive Management
The CEO is responsible for the long-term stability and competitiveness of the company. High employee turnover directly affects productivity and operational continuity.

**Needs**
- better visibility on workforce risks
- strategic insights on employee retention
- responsible and compliant use of AI technologies

### IT / Data Team
The IT team develops and maintains the technical infrastructure of the solution.

**Responsibilities**
- ensure system performance and reliability
- guarantee compliance with data protection regulations
- maintain transparency in AI model behavior

---

## Ethical and Regulatory Considerations

Because the company operates within the European regulatory framework, particular attention was given to compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

The system follows the principle of data minimization and privacy-by-design.

Personal identifiers such as manager names were removed from the model to limit privacy risks.

The processing of HR data is based on the legitimate interest of the company in improving employee retention and workplace well-being, while respecting employees' rights and freedoms.

To ensure transparency, employees will be informed about the system and how their data is used. When consent is required, it will follow the GDPR principles that consent must be:

- freely given
- specific
- informed
- unambiguous
