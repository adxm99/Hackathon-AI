# 📄 Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA)

**Project:** Employee Attrition Risk Prediction Tool
**Company Context:** IT Department – Automotive Component Manufacturing (Europe)

---

## 1. 🎯 Purpose Test — Identifying the Legitimate Interest

The company aims to develop a data-driven tool to **identify employees at risk of voluntary departure** in order to:

* Improve employee well-being and engagement
* Enable timely, supportive HR interventions
* Reduce operational disruption linked to attrition
* Preserve critical technical expertise in a highly specialized industrial environment

This objective constitutes a **legitimate business interest**, aligned with both organizational performance and employee support.

---

## 2. ⚙️ Necessity Test — Why is the Processing Needed?

The processing involves analyzing existing HR data to detect patterns associated with attrition risk.

This is considered necessary because:

* Early warning signals of disengagement are not easily detectable through manual processes
* The model enables **proactive rather than reactive HR actions**
* Only **relevant and limited variables** are used, including:

  * Organizational data (Department, Position, Seniority)
  * Engagement indicators (EngagementSurvey, EmpSatisfaction)
  * Career-related data (SpecialProjectsCount)
  * Compensation (Salary, in bands)
  * Performance trends (not raw evaluations)

The following data has been **excluded** to ensure proportionality:

* Direct identifiers (e.g., manager names)
* Highly intrusive behavioral tracking (e.g., detailed lateness or absence monitoring)

No less intrusive method has been identified that would achieve a similar level of insight.

---

## 3. ⚖️ Balancing Test — Impact on Employees

### Potential risks:

* Perception of monitoring or profiling
* Risk of bias or unfair targeting
* Misinterpretation of model outputs

### Mitigating safeguards:

* The system is strictly a **decision-support tool** (no automated decisions)
* Outputs are used only for **supportive HR actions**, not disciplinary measures
* Model decisions are **explainable** (no black-box logic)
* Regular **bias and fairness audits** are conducted
* Data is minimized and, where possible, aggregated
* Employees are **informed transparently** about the system
* Employees can exercise their rights under the General Data Protection Regulation, including the **right to object**

---

## 4. ✅ Conclusion

The processing is **lawful under the legitimate interest basis**, as:

* The purpose is valid and beneficial to both the company and employees
* The data used is limited to what is necessary
* The impact on individuals is minimized through strong safeguards

The company concludes that its legitimate interest **is not overridden by the rights and freedoms of employees**, provided that all safeguards are maintained and regularly reviewed.
